  • Stereo calibration of Minoru Camera

    My research thesis focuses on hand pose estimation from stereo capture. One of the fundamental issues to be addressed in solving this issue is depth recovery. Based on the concept of 'Stereopsis', in a stereo rig set up, the greater the shift in the observed location of an object the closer such object is to the stereo camera rig. This reduces the problem of depth recovery given stereo capture to establishing correspondence point on images captures by the pair of cameras on the stereo rig. The resulting image, whereby each pixel values indicate the separation between the pixelâ€™s location and the location of its correspondence, is referred to as disparity. To improve the speed and integrity of the correspondence search, the search space can be reduced from 2D to a 1D by ensuring the both cameras on the stereo rig are oriented such that they are perfectly parallel.

    Whilst it is impractical to achieve this physically, corrections can be made matheically to the captured images to give the appearnace of a perfectly parallel orientation. This process is referred to as Rectification. One way of achieving rectification is to establish the intrinsic (projective) and extrinsic (relative rotation and translation between pair of stereo camera). This requires the calibration of the stereo rig. This project implements the C++/OpenCV framework for calibrating stereo rigs and does this using the cheap and commercially avialable Minoru stereo camera.

    More details can be found in Chapter 3 of my thesis .
